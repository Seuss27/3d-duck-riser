### **Slicing & Printing Advice for the Duck Riser**

**1\. Orientation & Supports**

* **Orientation:** Print it exactly as oriented in the file—flat on its base, right-side up.  
* **Supports: OFF.** The internal cavity is mathematically sloped at 45 degrees to be completely self-supporting. Turning on auto-supports will unnecessarily fill the inside and waste a massive amount of filament.

**2\. Walls & Infill**

* **Perimeters (Walls):** Set your walls to **3 or 4 perimeters**. Since the wall thickness is set to 2.4mm, extra perimeters will make the dovetail joints and steps significantly stronger than relying on infill.  
* **Infill:** 10-15% Gyroid or Cubic is plenty. Most of the model is hollow anyway, so infill will mainly just support the solid reinforcement columns behind the dovetails.

**3\. Adhesion & Tolerances**

* **Elephant Foot:** The dovetails have a precise 0.25mm clearance. Be careful of "elephant foot" (first layer squish). If your first layer squishes out too far, you may need to take a hobby knife to the bottom edge of the dovetails so they slide together smoothly.  
* **Brim:** The footprint is large (approx 186x175mm). If you struggle with warping on large prints, use a brim—but be prepared to trim it very cleanly out of the female dovetail grooves.

**4\. Material & Cooling**

* **Material:** Standard PLA is perfect for this, assuming it will be kept indoors or in a climate-controlled cruise cabin.  
* **Cooling:** Ensure your part cooling fan is at 100% for the upper layers to cleanly bridge the peaks of the internal 45-degree "tents" under each step.
